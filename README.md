# 017-Supermarket-Sales-Analysis-SQL-Project

📊 Sales Performance Analytics Using SQL
📌 Project Overview

This project showcases SQL-based Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA) on a retail sales dataset to uncover actionable business insights related to sales performance, customer behavior, product trends, regional performance, and employee productivity.

🎯 Project Objectives
Analyze company sales performance over time.
Identify top-performing and underperforming products.
Evaluate product demand and seasonality.
Understand customer purchasing behavior.
Measure regional sales performance.
Assess employee sales productivity.
Discover business growth opportunities.
Demonstrate advanced SQL analytics techniques.

📊 Business Questions Solved
💰 Sales Performance Analysis

1. Highest revenue-generating products
Identifies products contributing the most revenue.

2. Lowest-performing products
Determines products with the weakest sales performance.

3. Monthly sales trends
Tracks revenue growth across months and years.

4. Highest sales month
Identifies peak-performing sales periods.

5. Average monthly order value
Measures average revenue generated per order each month.

6. Month-over-month sales growth
Uses window functions to calculate monthly growth rates and classify sales as growing or declining.

7. Products with consistent monthly growth
Identifies products demonstrating sustainable sales growth.

8. Seasonal products
Measures fluctuations in product sales to identify seasonality.

9. Highest revenue-generating categories
Ranks product categories by revenue contribution.

10. Underperforming categories
Highlights categories requiring business attention.

📦 Product Analysis

11. Products purchased in high quantities
Determines which products have the highest demand.

12. Products with declining demand
Tracks products experiencing decreasing monthly demand.

13. Products contributing the highest sales volume
Ranks products based on total quantity sold.

14. Fastest-growing product categories
Measures category growth using month-over-month growth rates.

15. Products recommended for promotion
Identifies products with low sales volume but strong revenue potential.

16. Products for possible discontinuation
Highlights consistently low-performing products.

17. Products with stable demand
Measures demand consistency across multiple months.

👥 Customer Analysis

18. Highest-value customers
Ranks customers by total revenue generated.

19. Regions with the largest customer base
Identifies customer concentration by region.

20. Customer spending by age group
Compares purchasing behavior across different age segments.

21. Purchasing behavior by gender
Analyzes differences in product purchases between male and female customers.

22. Highest revenue-generating region
Measures regional sales performance.

23. Average customer spending
Calculates average lifetime customer spending.

24. Most frequent customers
Identifies customers with the highest purchase frequency.

🌎 Regional Business Analysis

25. Region with the highest average sales value
Evaluates regional revenue performance.

26. Regions with low sales but high customer counts
Identifies regions with untapped revenue opportunities.

👨‍💼 Employee Performance Analysis

27. Highest revenue-generating employees
Ranks employees based on sales revenue.

28. Employees processing the highest order volumes
Measures employee productivity through quantities sold.

📈 Advanced Business Analytics

29. Price vs. quantity relationship
Calculates the Pearson Correlation Coefficient to determine whether pricing influences product demand.

30. Revenue-driving business factors
Analyzes the combined impact of:
Product Category
Customer Region
Order Volume
Quantity Sold
Unit Price
Average Order Value
to determine the strongest contributors to sales revenue.

⭐ Key Takeaways

This project demonstrates how SQL can transform transactional sales data into strategic business insights. Through comprehensive analyses of sales trends, product performance, customer behavior, regional markets, and employee productivity, it showcases practical SQL skills used in real-world analytics. By applying advanced techniques such as CTEs, window functions, subqueries, and correlation analysis, the project highlights the value of SQL for supporting data-driven decision-making and business intelligence. It serves as a strong portfolio project for roles such as Data Analyst, Business Intelligence Analyst, SQL Developer, Sales Analyst, and Business Analyst.
